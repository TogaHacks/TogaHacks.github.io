Frequently Asked Questions

What is togaHacks?

togaHacks is the first fully student-run hackathon from Saratoga High School.
It is a 24 hour event where high school students from all around can code a simple idea
of a game or application into reality. At the end of the event, teams will pitch their project
to a panel of esteemed judges. For this event, we are hoping to have 200 to
250 participants at Saratoga!

Who can participate in the event?

To participate in the event, you must be a current high school student.
In other words, the oldest people elligible to participate in the evnt will
be those graduating in the Class of 2018.

How much does it cost to enter?

To help cover costs for all individuals, such as food, prizes, and swag,
we will be charging each participant an entry fee of $10. You can pay us at
the time of the event, or pay us online through our PayPal portal.

Do I need to bring anything to the event?

You will want to bring your printed admission ticket, payment if
paying at the door, and of course, your laptop, charger, and other
peripheral devices for all your hackathon needs!

How do I form or join a team?

You can pre-form a team and attend as a group of friends, or you
can come with ideas to our pitch presentation, which will take place
during the opening ceremony. We will also create a channel on Slack in
order to ask questions, post general announcements about the event,
and communicate with sponsors about their APIs.

What if I can't code?

If you can't code, don't sweat it! We will have workshops to learn
code languages, and you'll also pick up different skills during your time at togaHacks.
Also, hackathons aren't purely code - Artists, and those who can design UIs/UXs are
also very helpful for teams in hackathons, who want to make their product
not only successful, but also good-looking!

Do you have a set of rules to follow?

Yes. To participate in the event, you must agree to a code of conduct.
If any of the rules in the code of conduct book are broken, you assume risk
of your entire team being disqualified from winning any prizes.
